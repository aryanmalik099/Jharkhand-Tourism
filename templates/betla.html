Journey into Betla's Heart

Betla National Park, nestled in the picturesque Palamu hills of Jharkhand, stands as one of India's pioneering tiger reserves. Spanning diverse terrains from dense tropical forests to cascading waterfalls, it's a vibrant ecosystem teeming with life. This sanctuary is a true jewel for wildlife enthusiasts and a beacon for those seeking tranquility amidst nature.

Beyond the majestic tigers, Betla is a stronghold for Asian elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and an impressive array of deer species. Its skies are graced by over 170 species of birds, making it a birder's paradise where even rare hornbills can be spotted.

Experience the Best of Betla

Thrilling Wildlife Safaris

Embark on guided jeep safaris through the core zones, offering unparalleled chances to encounter tigers, elephants, and other magnificent wildlife in their natural habitat.

Enchanting Waterfalls & Trails

Discover the breathtaking beauty of Lodh Falls, Jharkhand's highest waterfall, and explore serene forest trails ideal for nature walks and photography.

Ancient Palamu Forts

Delve into history within the park's boundaries by visiting the majestic Palamu Forts, ancient structures that tell tales of erstwhile dynasties.

Birdwatcher's Paradise

With over 170 avian species, Betla offers a fantastic opportunity for birdwatching, from vibrant kingfishers to elusive hornbills.
